Healthy Snacks for Weight Gain

Do you want to know the kind of snacks Dietitian Michelle recommends for weight gain? Natural, nutritious, and delicious snacks you can enjoy while working towards your weight goals.

Understanding Healthy Weight Gain

Many people struggle with being underweight, which can be just as challenging as losing weight. Healthy weight gain isn't about eating junk food or empty calories‚Äîit's about nutrient-dense foods that support your overall health while helping you reach your weight goals.

üìä Key Principle

Focus on calorie-dense but nutrient-rich foods that provide energy, protein, healthy fats, and essential vitamins and minerals.

Top Recommended Snacks for Weight Gain

ü•ú Nut and Seed-Based Snacks

Nuts and seeds are calorie-dense and packed with healthy fats, protein, and fiber.

Almond Butter on Whole Grain Toast

Spread 2 tablespoons of almond butter on whole grain toast. Add sliced bananas and a drizzle of honey for extra calories and flavor.

~350 calories

Trail Mix

Mix almonds, walnuts, cashews, dried fruits, and dark chocolate chips. Perfect for on-the-go snacking.

~200 calories per 1/4 cup

Chia Seed Pudding

Soak chia seeds in full-fat coconut milk overnight. Top with berries and granola for a nutrient-packed snack.

~400 calories per serving

ü•õ Dairy and Protein-Rich Snacks

These snacks provide high-quality protein and healthy fats for muscle building and weight gain.

Greek Yogurt Parfait

Full-fat Greek yogurt layered with granola, nuts, and honey. Rich in protein and calories.

~300-400 calories

Cottage Cheese with Fruit

Full-fat cottage cheese topped with pineapple chunks or peaches and a sprinkle of nuts.

~250-300 calories

Protein Smoothie

Blend whole milk, banana, peanut butter, protein powder, and oats for a calorie-dense drink.

~500-600 calories

üç† Carbohydrate-Based Snacks

Complex carbohydrates provide sustained energy and help in healthy weight gain.

Sweet Potato with Toppings

Baked sweet potato topped with butter, Greek yogurt, or avocado for extra calories.

~300-400 calories

Whole Grain Crackers with Hummus

Pair whole grain crackers with generous servings of hummus and olive oil.

~250-350 calories

Oatmeal with Toppings

Prepare oatmeal with whole milk and top with nuts, seeds, dried fruits, and honey.

~400-500 calories

üíß Stay Hydrated

Drink fluids between meals rather than with meals to avoid filling up on liquids.

Final Thoughts

Healthy weight gain is a journey that requires patience and consistency. By choosing nutrient-dense snacks and eating regularly throughout the day, you can achieve your weight goals while supporting your overall health.

Remember, the key is to focus on quality calories that provide your body with the nutrients it needs to thrive.
